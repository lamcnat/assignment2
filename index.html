About the Collection

Discover the timeless artistry of Jingdezhen porcelain, known for its delicate craftsmanship and rich history. Explore the beauty of traditional Chinese ceramics in a modern context.

Jingdezhen Ware

All the pieces originate from Jingdezhen, Jiangxi province, the renowned center of porcelain production in China. They showcase the high-quality porcelain and cobalt blue underglaze characteristic of the period.

Kangxi Style

The pieces reflect the aesthetic sensibilities of the Kangxi reign, featuring motifs like flowers, auspicious symbols, and scenes of leisure.

Cobalt Blue Decoration

All pieces utilize cobalt blue underglaze decoration, a defining feature of blue and white porcelain from this period.

Diversity of Forms

The collection includes dishes, vases, and a jar with lid, demonstrating the range of forms produced at Jingdezhen.

Bottle-shaped vase (F1982.19a-b)

A Qing dynasty bottle-shaped vase from Jingdezhen, featuring cobalt blue underglaze painting of rockery with flowering plants. Created during the Kangxi reign (1662-1722). ...

Type: Vessel
Date: 1662-1722
Period: Qing dynasty, Kangxi reign
Origin: Jingdezhen, Jiangxi province, China
Style: Jingdezhen ware
Medium: Porcelain with cobalt pigment under clear colourless glaze
Dimensions: Height × Diameter: 18.8 × 10 cm (7 3/8 × 3 15/16 in)

Details:
This bottle-shaped vase has a pear shape with a long, tapering neck and is one of a pair with F1982.20. Made of white porcelain with clear glaze, it's decorated with underglaze cobalt blue painting. The body features rockery with flowering plants, with the ground filled with foliate floral scrolls. The neck is adorned with flowering branches and hatched borders, finished with brown glaze on the lip.
